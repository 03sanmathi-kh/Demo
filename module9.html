Module 8: Worker Knowledge Transfer System

Problem Statement

In Vishalanagari's bustling factories and service hubs, invaluable tacit knowledge — quick fixes, machine-specific tips, and local heuristics — evaporates during shift handovers. Incoming workers waste time rediscovering solutions, errors reappear, and overall throughput suffers. We need a gentle, intelligent keeper of workplace wisdom that captures on-the-floor insights in real-time, organizes them for instant lookup, and hands the right guidance to the next person on duty. The system should prioritize critical notes, surface recent shift highlights, and make finding relevant knowledge as natural as typing a single keyword. The result: fewer repeated mistakes, faster problem resolution, and a workforce that learns as it works.

Algorithms Used

Trie (Prefix Tree): The Trie acts as the instant dictionary of shop-floor wisdom. By indexing keywords and procedure fragments, it lets workers retrieve relevant advice with partial inputs — type “vib” and see everything about vibrations — making lookup immediate and typo-tolerant.

Skip List (Timestamped): The Skip List maintains a fast, chronological log of shift notes and updates. Because insertions and recent-lookups are common during handovers, the Skip List gives O(log n) access while preserving the “what happened last” view that incoming workers rely on.

Red-Black Tree: The Red-Black Tree stores validated insights sorted by priority, severity, or frequency so critical notes surface quickly. Its guaranteed balancing ensures that queries for top-priority issues remain fast even as the knowledge base grows large.

Algorithm Efficiency

Trie: Insert & search in O(m) where m = length of the keyword or phrase.

Skip List: Insert / search / delete in expected O(log n), ideal for dynamic logs.

Red-Black Tree: Insert / delete / search guaranteed O(log n), excellent for balanced priority access.

How This Aligns with SDGs

SDG 8 — Decent Work & Economic Growth: Preserves practical skills and reduces downtime, helping workers perform better and industries run smoother.

SDG 9 — Industry, Innovation & Infrastructure: Builds robust digital infrastructure for knowledge continuity and resilient operations.

SDG 4 — Quality Education: Encourages on-the-job learning by surfacing proven solutions and tacit expertise to all workers.

C++ Implementation
Two integrated implementations are provided to demonstrate different architectural trade-offs. The primary build (Trie + Skip List + Red-Black Tree) focuses on fast keyword lookup, chronological logs, and balanced priority access — ideal for production deployments where both recency and priority matter. The alternate build (Trie + 2-3 Tree + Skip List) emphasizes textbook-balanced storage for category-grouped knowledge while keeping quick shift logs, offering a more classical data-structure flavor for academic comparison.
