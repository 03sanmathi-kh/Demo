Module 4: Water Supply Network Optimization in Vishalanagri

Problem Statement

Vishalanagri depends on the Purna River for supplying water to multiple treatment plants, residential zones, and industrial areas. Designing an efficient pipeline network is challenging due to varying elevations, urban density, and fluctuating demand during peak hours. The goal is to construct a cost-effective, reliable network that maintains stable pressure, ensures emergency redundancy, and minimizes water loss. Graph-based algorithms help optimize routing and connectivity while keeping costs low and flow efficient.

Algorithms Used

1. Prim’s Algorithm + Dijkstra: Prim’s constructs a minimum spanning tree (MST) connecting all treatment plants and zones to minimize pipeline costs. Dijkstra is applied afterward to compute shortest paths from each water source, ensuring efficient water distribution and stable pressure.

2. Kruskal’s Algorithm + Dijkstra: Kruskal builds an MST by sorting edges and avoiding cycles. Combined with Dijkstra, it ensures shortest paths for water delivery while checking network redundancy and efficiency.

3. BFS (Optional): BFS explores the network level-wise to visualize reachability and identifies which zones can be supplied first during emergencies. (Not fully realistic for pressure optimization, but good for reachability mapping.)

Efficiency of Algorithms

Prim’s Algorithm: O(E log V) – Efficient MST construction.

Kruskal’s Algorithm: O(E log V) – Alternative MST construction with cycle detection.

Dijkstra: O((V + E) log V) – Computes shortest paths across weighted network.

BFS: O(V + E) – Level-wise network traversal to check reachability.
