Module 1:Smart irrigation and water distribution netwrok

Problem Statement

The Smart Irrigation and Water Distribution Network aims to ensure efficient and reliable water supply to agricultural lands in Vishalnagari. Traditional irrigation systems often result in water wastage and uneven distribution. This module models reservoirs, pipelines, and farmlands as interconnected components to optimize water flow. The objective is to minimize water loss, reduce distribution time, and ensure equitable water delivery to all farms.

Algorithms Used

1. Graphs: Farm as vertices, pipeline as edges.

2.BFS : Lvel wise water distibution.

3. DFS: Leakage or connectivity check.

4. dijkstra's Algorithm:shorter water path.

Algorithm Efficiency

  • Greedy Allocation: O(n log n) – efficient for real-time assignment of materials.
  • Priority Queues: O(log n) per operation – manages factory priority efficiently.
  • Sliding Window Forecasting: O(n) – calculates predictions over recent data with minimal overhead.
