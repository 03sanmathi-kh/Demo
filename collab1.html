Collaboration – Vishalanagri Project

Sanmathi & Akshata Collaboration

Focus Areas:

• Sanmathi: Industries, Infrastructure, Logistics Optimization

• Akshata: Traffic, Transport, Flow Efficiency

Project Goal:

• In Vishalanagri, cotton harvested in the early morning from different farm clusters often reaches nearby ginning mills late due to road bottlenecks and unpredictable traffic. This delay causes processing queues, factory slowdowns, and at times even shipment backlogs at export terminals. Our goal is to build a system that can predict these inefficiencies before they occur and redirect vehicles through alternate routes in real time.

• The project aims to create an end-to-end intelligent network where cotton moves smoothly from farms → mills → textile factories → ports. The algorithms help identify the fastest and cheapest route at every hour of the day by analysing road load, distance, cost, and congestion. When traffic spikes unexpectedly, the system adjusts instantly, ensuring trucks keep moving without getting stuck.

• Ultimately, the project ensures that industries never run out of raw cotton, mills maintain uninterrupted operations, factories ship goods on schedule, and the entire city experiences reduced congestion because transport flows are evenly distributed.

• This creates a fully optimized cotton supply chain where farmers get faster returns, industries get steady supply, transport becomes more predictable, and environmental impact is reduced through efficient route usage.

Algorithms Used:

• Dijkstra: Minimum-cost paths

• Bellman-Ford: Handles negative edge weights, detects cycles

• Floyd-Warshall: All-pairs shortest paths

• Min Cost Max Flow: Optimal cotton distribution

Benefits:

• Reduced transportation costs

• Faster delivery & no bottlenecks

• Sustainable & lower emissions

• Load balancing across routes
