Healthcare Appointment Optimization

Problem Statement

In an Ayushman Bharat health camp set up in a semi-urban region, a limited number of doctors are available for a fixed duration each day. Patients arrive with different medical needs, and each consultation requires a specific time interval.

Since doctor availability is limited, not every patient can be treated. The challenge is to schedule appointments in such a way that the **maximum number of patients** receive treatment without overlapping time slots.

The system must ensure efficient utilization of doctor time while maintaining fairness and avoiding appointment conflicts.

Algorithm Used

Interval Scheduling Algorithm

  • Each patient appointment is treated as a time interval.
  • Appointments are sorted based on their finishing times.
  • Patients are selected such that no two appointments overlap.
  • This ensures maximum patient coverage within available time.

Algorithm Efficiency: O(n log n) due to sorting of appointment intervals

Design Techniques Used

  • Greedy Choice: Select earliest finishing appointment
  • Sorting: Orders intervals efficiently
  • Decomposition: Divides scheduling into simple steps
  • Abstraction: Represents patients as time intervals

Relevant Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)

  • SDG 3 – Good Health and Well-being: Improves access to healthcare
  • SDG 10 – Reduced Inequalities: Fair distribution of medical services
  • SDG 9 – Industry, Innovation & Infrastructure: Efficient system design
