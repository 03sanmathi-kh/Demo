Digital Connectivity Optimization in Vishalanagari

Problem Statement

In bustling public spaces like tourist hubs, stadiums, and transport terminals, digital networks often crumble under sudden surges of user demand. Access points (APs) get congested, bandwidth is unevenly distributed, and users experience lag or dropped connections. This module envisions an intelligent connectivity system that anticipates spikes, dynamically routes traffic, enforces fair bandwidth allocation, and maintains seamless internet access for everyone—even at the busiest moments.

Algorithm 1: Min-Cost Max-Flow

Purpose: Globally optimizes traffic flow across APs to maximize total throughput and minimize congestion by modeling the network as a capacitated graph, where each edge has bandwidth capacity and cost (latency).

Efficiency: O(F * (E log V)) — F = total flow, E = edges, V = vertices

Algorithm 2: Segment Tree with Lazy Propagation

Purpose: Maintains per-AP or per-zone bandwidth quotas. Handles fast range updates to dynamically control traffic during sudden bursts, ensuring no AP exceeds capacity while maintaining efficiency.

Efficiency: Updates: O(log n), Range Queries: O(log n)

Algorithm 3: Skip List

Purpose: Tracks active sessions by priority or demand. Enables quick reassignment of sessions when AP conditions change. Simpler than balanced trees but provides strong average-case performance.

Efficiency: Expected O(log n) for insert, delete, and search
